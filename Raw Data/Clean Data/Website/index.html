California Housing Element Analysis and Prediction

In California, it is required that local governments (such as cities and counties) have adequate plans in place to meet the housing needs of their communities. These plans are called "general plans" and are a part of the local government's overall plan for growth and development. General plans include eight elements, including land use, transportation, conservation, noise, open space, safety, environmental justice, and housing. Local governments must adopt these plans in order to ensure that the private market can meet the housing needs and demand of Californians. The effectiveness of local housing policy in California depends on the implementation of these general plans and local housing elements.

Why This Project?

According to a study published in the Journal of Urban Economics, the high demand for housing in California, combined with a lack of supply, has contributed to the high cost of housing in the state. This has led to an increase in rent and home prices, making it difficult for many people to afford a place to live.

Additionally, a report from the California Housing Partnership Corporation found that the shortage of affordable housing in the state has had a particularly negative impact on low-income individuals and families. Many have been forced to spend a disproportionate amount of their income on housing, leaving them with less money for other necessities such as food and healthcare. This has contributed to financial stress and insecurity, as well as an increase in homelessness.
